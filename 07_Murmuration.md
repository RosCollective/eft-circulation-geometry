# 7. Murmuration

Murmuration describes the emergence of distributed coherence across the field.

Where propagation spreads consequence, redistribution reorganizes load, and resonance amplifies intensity, murmuration organizes coherence without central control.

Murmuration is not alignment.
It is not synchronization.
It is not collective motion.
It is coherence emerging from distributed responsiveness.

Regions respond to local conditions.
Their responses influence adjacent regions.
Patterns of responsiveness begin to stabilize.
Coherence emerges across the field.

Murmuration begins with local adjustments.
Small shifts.
Micro-orientations.
Changes in sensitivity.
Minor redistributions of attention and load.

No region leads.
No region commands.
No region holds the pattern.
The pattern emerges from the relations themselves.

As responsiveness accumulates, coherence becomes visible.
Shared orientations appear.
Coherent flows emerge.
Distributed responsiveness becomes organized without becoming centralized.
The field becomes coherent without becoming uniform.

Murmuration preserves integrity by allowing the field to act as a whole while remaining distributed.

Integrity remains visible through emergent coherence.

Murmuration is the morphology through which a field organizes itself without requiring control.

It is the fourth core expression of circulation.
