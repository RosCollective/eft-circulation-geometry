# 1. Recognition of Circulation

The earliest work began with mobility.

This led to the recognition of **Integrity of Motion**: a condition in which movement remains coherent because each unit carries structural load along an active trajectory.

Mobility Geometry emerged from Integrity of Motion. It described how movement forms, bends, collapses, and reconstitutes within a coherent field.

For a long time, this framework was sufficient.

As observations accumulated, however, the field began exhibiting behaviors that did not resemble trajectory alone.

Integrity remained visible through motion, but motion was not the only form through which integrity could persist.

Activity appeared in more than one place without traveling along a path.
Load reorganized without returning to a prior form.
Coherence persisted despite local variation.
Movement remained meaningful even when no single vector could be identified.

These observations did not contradict Motion Geometry.

They suggested that integrity might persist through additional mechanisms.

Integrity remained visible.

The morphology had changed.

The recognition was gradual.

Motion Geometry remained accurate for the phenomena it described.
Yet the field was exhibiting behaviors that could not be understood through motion alone.

Activity was not only moving.
It was circulating.

The chapters that follow describe this morphology and the forms through which it becomes visible.
