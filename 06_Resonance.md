# 6. Resonance

Resonance describes how activity in one region amplifies activity in another without requiring direct connection.

Where propagation spreads consequence and redistribution reorganizes load, resonance increases intensity across the field.

Resonance is not spread.
It is not rebalancing.
It is amplification.
A region intensifies.
Another region responds with increased intensity.
The amplification becomes mutual.
Activity strengthens across distance.

The regions do not require adjacency.
They do not require alignment.
They do not require a path.
Resonance emerges through relation rather than proximity.

As responsiveness increases, amplification becomes self-reinforcing.

One region intensifies another.
The intensified region amplifies the first in return.
The field becomes more responsive as a whole.

A field in resonance often feels different.

Attention deepens.
Responsiveness increases.
The space between becomes active.
The field develops charge.
Something is happening, and the participants can feel it.

Resonance preserves integrity by increasing the field's capacity to respond without requiring central coordination.

Integrity remains visible through mutual amplification.

Resonance is the morphology through which a circulating field becomes visibly alive.

It is the third core expression of circulation.
