# 4. Propagation

Propagation is the process by which a local change becomes a field-wide consequence through successive relational responses.

A region changes.
Adjacent regions adjust.
Their adjustment alters conditions for others.
The consequence spreads across the field.

Propagation does not describe movement.
It describes how consequence becomes nonlocal.

Nothing is transmitted from one region to another.
The field reorganizes because its regions are in relation.

A local event becomes a field event.

Propagation preserves integrity when consequence continues without requiring a single path, source, or center.

The field remains responsive.
The consequence remains meaningful.
The morphology remains alive.

Propagation is the simplest expression of circulation.

The chapters that follow describe increasingly complex forms of the same underlying behavior.
