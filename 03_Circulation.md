# 3. Circulation

Circulation is a morphology in which integrity is preserved through distributed responsiveness rather than directional movement.

In Motion Geometry, integrity appeared through trajectory. Structural load was carried along an active path. Coherence emerged through directional continuity.

Circulation preserves integrity differently.

Activity may intensify across multiple regions without traveling along a single route.
Load may reorganize without returning to a prior form.
Coherence may emerge without alignment.
Participation may shift without collapse.

In each case, integrity remains visible.

What changes is the mechanism through which it is preserved.

Circulation does not organize around a path.
It organizes around relation.

Consequence moves through responsiveness rather than displacement.
Coherence emerges through participation rather than alignment.

The morphology remains stable not because it returns to a previous state, but because it continually adapts to changing conditions.

Several recurring expressions of circulation became visible through observation.

Propagation describes the movement of consequence through relation.

Redistribution describes the reorganization of load under changing conditions.

Resonance describes amplification across a responsive field.

Murmuration describes the emergence of distributed coherence without central control.

These expressions are distinct, but they share a common morphology.
Together they reveal circulation as a persistent mode through which integrity can be maintained under change.

The chapters that follow examine each expression in detail.
