# 2. Motion and Circulation
## The Two Morphologies of Integrity

Motion and circulation describe two distinct ways the field preserves integrity under change.

Both are observable.
Both preserve integrity under change.
They differ in mechanism.

Motion preserves integrity through directional advancement.
Circulation preserves integrity through distributed responsiveness.

Motion organizes around a path.
Circulation organizes around a field.

### Motion

Motion is characterized by:

- trajectory
- curvature
- collapse
- reconstitution
- alignment
- stability

Motion preserves integrity when structural load is carried along an active trajectory.
The path may bend, compress, fragment, or recover, but integrity remains visible as long as meaningful carrying continues.

Motion is a morphology of continuity through direction.

### Circulation

Circulation is characterized by:

- propagation
- redistribution
- resonance
- murmuration

Circulation preserves integrity when consequence moves through relation rather than displacement.
Coherence emerges through responsiveness rather than alignment.
Load adapts rather than returns.
Participation shifts without collapse.

Circulation is a morphology of continuity through relation.

### Distinction

Motion and circulation preserve integrity differently.

Motion depends upon directional continuity.
Circulation depends upon relational continuity.

Motion is defined by what moves.
Circulation is defined by what responds.

Motion stabilizes through reconstitution.
Circulation sustains through redistribution.

Neither replaces the other.

Each becomes visible under different conditions.

### Integrity Across Morphologies

Integrity is the invariant across both morphologies.

In motion, integrity appears as meaningful carrying along a trajectory.
In circulation, integrity appears as meaningful consequence distributed through relation.

The mechanisms differ.
The condition does not.

Motion and circulation are two observable morphologies through which integrity becomes visible.
